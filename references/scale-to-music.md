# Scale-to-Music Integration

Never leave scale work abstract. Every scale block should connect to one musical output.

## Required outputs
- A short lick
- A riff fragment
- A 30 to 60 second improv prompt
- A matching chord progression

## Major scale ideas
- Use 1-2-3 and 5 for simple melodic hooks.
- Land on the root or 3rd when the chord changes.

## Minor pentatonic ideas
- Build small call-and-response phrases.
- Use bends, slides, and rests to keep the phrase musical.

## Major pentatonic ideas
- Keep the line open and singable.
- Avoid overfilling every beat.

## Natural minor ideas
- Use the full 7-note scale but start with melodic fragments of 4–5 notes.
- Descend from the octave to the 5th as a go-to phrase shape.
- Target chord tones on beats 1 and 3 when playing over i-bVII-bVI.

## Blues scale ideas
- Treat the b5 as a chromatic passing tone between 4 and 5, not a landing note.
- Emphasize the b5 as a bend (4 bent up to b5) for tension.
- Resolve phrases to the root or 5th of each chord in the 12-bar form.

## Dorian mode ideas
- The natural 6 (raised 6th compared to natural minor) is the signature color note — feature it.
- Use a i-IV vamp (minor i, major IV built on the b7) as the home progression.
- Build riffs from 1, b3, 5, and 6 to emphasize the dorian sound.

## Mixolydian mode ideas
- The b7 is the color note — include it in licks and riffs.
- Use 1, 3, 5, b7 as a chord-tone framework for melodic ideas.
- Play over a I7 chord or a I7-bVII-IV-I progression for the authentic mixolydian feel.
