# Coaching Modes

Use this reference to decide which session shape fits the user's readiness and recent progress.

## Readiness-driven modes

- **full**: Use when time, energy, focus, and comfort are all acceptable.
- **review-only**: Use when focus is low or recent logs show overload. Keep the session on familiar material plus one small cleanup target.
- **low-friction**: Use when motivation or available time is low. Prioritize one easy win and one habit-preserving block.
- **recovery**: Use when pain or tension is elevated. Reduce difficulty immediately and keep the work technique-safe.

## Repair mode

Use a fix-one-thing session when one repeated issue keeps appearing across logs. Keep the session narrow and concrete.

## Plateau rules

A plateau is likely when the same issue keeps appearing for at least half of the last 6 to 8 logs and advancement is rare. When that happens:

1. simplify the task
2. reduce tempo or range
3. shorten the loop
4. require one clear mini-win before re-expanding

## Progress review

Use progress charts for motivation and weekly reviews. Focus on practice minutes, overall rating trend, and how often the logs end with `#status/advance`.
