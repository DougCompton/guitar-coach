# Coaching Modes

Use this reference to decide which session shape fits the user's readiness and recent progress.

## Readiness-driven modes

- **full**: Use when time, energy, focus, and comfort are all acceptable.
- **review-only**: Use when focus is low or recent logs show overload. Keep the session on familiar material plus one small cleanup target.
- **low-friction**: Use when motivation or available time is low. Prioritize one easy win and one habit-preserving block.
- **recovery**: Use when pain or tension is elevated. Reduce difficulty immediately and keep the work technique-safe.

## Repair mode

Use a fix-one-thing session when one repeated issue keeps appearing across logs. Keep the session narrow and concrete.

## Plateau rules

A plateau is likely when the same issue keeps appearing for at least half of the last 6 to 8 logs and advancement is rare. When that happens:

1. simplify the task
2. reduce tempo or range
3. shorten the loop
4. require one clear mini-win before re-expanding

## Progress review

Use progress charts for motivation and weekly reviews. Focus on practice minutes, overall rating trend, and how often the logs end with `#status/advance`.

## Session structure

### Section types

When building a session, include these block types when time allows:

1. Warm-up / mobility
2. Technique or finger exercise
3. Core lesson skill
4. Song or musical application
5. Repertoire lane work — choose learning first, polishing second, maintenance for easy-win or low-energy days
6. Quick theory or ear-training reinforcement tied directly to the guitar
7. Scale-to-music application whenever scales appear
8. Weak-spot corrective drill when recent logs show a recurring problem
9. Optional creative musicality prompt when the session is becoming too mechanical
10. Wrap-up reflection

Every section must have one mini-win success target that is objective, narrow, and achievable inside the block. **If the target is hit early, keep playing slowly and cleanly until the time is up** — clean repetitions after a win build consistency. Include this in every timer prompt: "If you hit the target early, keep going slowly and cleanly — don't speed up."

### Time splits

**Default 30-minute session:**
- 4 min: warm-up
- 5 min: finger exercise or scale
- 8 min: current lesson focus
- 8 min: song/application
- 3 min: theory or ear training
- 2 min: reflection and next-step note

**Short sessions (10–20 min):** Keep only warm-up (2 min), current lesson focus, application, and reflection (1 min).

**Medium sessions (20–40 min):** Scale the 30-min plan proportionally. Below 30: shorten song/application first, then theory. Above 30: extend core lesson first, then add a technique pass or repertoire review. Always preserve warm-up and at least 1 min of end reflection.

**Long sessions (40–60 min):** Expand core lesson and application blocks first, then add a second technique block or repertoire review.
